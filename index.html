01 / Profile

I make things that
do not fail.

I am a rising third-year mechanical engineering student at NC State, graduating in 2028. One idea pulls my work together: the future runs on clean, cheap, reliable energy, and I want to build the hardware that delivers it.

That focus is not abstract. In a fusion lab I studied reactor wall stability. Leading Windpack, I learned reliability the hands-on way: build, test, find the failure, fix it. At GE Vernova I now work on wind hardware built to run in the field for decades.

Reliability engineering at SpaceX is the natural next step: the same discipline, pointed at the hardest mechanical problem there is. I also spent three years as a Wake County EMS cadet, which is a short way of saying I stay calm when the stakes are real.
